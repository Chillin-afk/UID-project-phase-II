Climate Details

Paris has a temperate oceanic climate (Köppen: Cfb), with mild winters and warm summers. The average yearly temperature is around 12.8°C. Rain is fairly well distributed throughout the year, with approximately 641 mm of precipitation annually. Occasional summer heatwaves and winter cold snaps can occur.

Wildlife & Nature

Paris is home to diverse urban wildlife including foxes, hedgehogs, ducks, pigeons, and even falcons nesting on cathedrals. Parks like the Buttes-Chaumont, Bois de Boulogne, and the Seine riverbanks provide important green corridors that support biodiversity within the city.

Seasonal Weather

Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant weather in Paris with blooming gardens and colorful leaves. Summers can be warm, occasionally hot during heatwaves. Winters are cold and damp with occasional snow, especially from December through February.

Interesting Weather Facts

Paris has experienced severe floods, such as the 1910 Great Flood and more recent events in 2016 and 2018, due to the Seine overflowing. The highest recorded temperature in Paris reached 42.6°C during the 2019 heatwave, while the lowest was -23.9°C in 1879.
