Climate Details

New York City experiences a humid subtropical climate, marked by hot and humid summers and cold, snowy winters. Average annual snowfall can exceed 60 inches in some years, blanketing the city in white during winter months.

The Atlantic Ocean influences the city’s temperatures and often brings nor’easters—intense storms characterized by heavy snow, rain, and wind.

Wildlife & Nature

Despite being a major metropolis, New York supports an impressive variety of wildlife. Peregrine falcons, red-tailed hawks, raccoons, and even coyotes have adapted to urban life.

Green spaces like Central Park and the Bronx River provide critical habitats for migratory birds and mammals, while the city's waterways are home to seals and occasional dolphins.

Seasonal Weather

Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant conditions in New York. Autumn in particular brings vibrant foliage across city parks, drawing tourists and locals alike.

Summer brings humidity and frequent thunderstorms, while winter brings freezing winds and occasional blizzards.

Interesting Weather Facts

NYC has microclimates due to its skyscrapers and urban heat island effect. Some neighborhoods are consistently warmer than others due to concentrated concrete and asphalt.

Major storms like Hurricane Sandy (2012) have shown the city’s vulnerability to climate change and sea-level rise, prompting new climate-resilient infrastructure projects.
