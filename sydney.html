Climate Details

Sydney has a humid subtropical climate (Köppen: Cfa), with warm summers and mild winters. Rain is well distributed throughout the year, but late summer and autumn can be particularly wet. Annual average temperature is around 18.5°C, with summer highs over 30°C and winter lows around 8°C.

Wildlife & Nature

Sydney’s bushlands, coastal reserves, and national parks are home to unique wildlife including kookaburras, possums, lorikeets, flying foxes, and water dragons. Nearby ocean waters are rich with dolphins, whales (seasonally), and penguins around coastal cliffs and islands.

Seasonal Weather

Summer in Sydney (Dec–Feb) is warm with occasional heatwaves. Autumn brings milder temperatures and colorful trees. Winters (Jun–Aug) are cool and pleasant with little frost. Spring brings blooming flowers and a return to warmer days, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Interesting Weather Facts

Sydney occasionally faces powerful thunderstorms and East Coast Lows, leading to flash flooding. The 2022 floods were among the worst in decades. The city also holds the record for Australia’s highest February temperature of 45.8°C, recorded in 2017.
